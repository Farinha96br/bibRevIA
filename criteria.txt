Goal: select papers for a survey on High-Performance Computing (HPC) applied to seismic inversion. Judge each paper using ONLY its title and abstract.

INCLUDE when the abstract contains BOTH (the paper does NOT need to focus on
HPC — it is enough that an HPC/computational topic is mentioned or cited):
  1. Full Waveform Inversion (FWI) or Reverse Time Migration (RTM); AND
  2. at least one HPC / computational topic mentioned, for example:
       - hardware: multi-core CPUs, GPUs/GPGPUs, APUs, TPUs, tensor cores, FPGAs, Cerebras, or other hardware accelerators;
       - parallel-programming / performance frameworks: CUDA, HIP, OpenMP, OpenACC, MPI, OpenCL, Kokkos, SYCL, Devito;
       - memory, storage and data movement: checkpointing, compression, out-of-core, decimation, I/O (input/output), disk/storage usage;
       - reduced/mixed precision: mixed, half, or low precision;
       - general: parallelization, performance, or scalability.

EXCLUDE when the abstract is missing either requirement, or when it is:
  - medical imaging;
  - machine learning;
  - seismic processing unrelated to FWI/RTM;
  - a case study on a specific geological site;
  - 1D or 2D only (2.5D is allowed);
  - not the actual abstract of an article (proceedings/conference listings, tables of contents, abstracts with no real article content).
